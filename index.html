About

I am a Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Kansas. My work is centered on mathematical modeling, estimation, and control of lithium-ion battery systems.

I am particularly interested in developing data-driven and physics-informed methods for battery system identification, parameter estimation, and forecasting. My current research uses state-space models and Ensemble Kalman Inversion to estimate parameters in nonlinear battery models with coupled electrochemical and thermal dynamics.

Research Interests

  • Lithium-ion battery modeling
  • System identification and parameter estimation
  • Ensemble Kalman Inversion and Bayesian inverse problems
  • State-space models and filtering methods
  • Control systems and estimation theory
  • Scientific machine learning for dynamical systems
