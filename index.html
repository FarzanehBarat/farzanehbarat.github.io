About

I work at the intersection of mechanical engineering, dynamical systems, uncertainty quantification, and scientific machine learning. My research is motivated by the need for reliable, interpretable, and physics-informed models for energy systems, especially lithium-ion batteries.

I am particularly interested in state-space modeling, filtering, Ensemble Kalman Inversion, nonlinear system identification, and forecasting methods that combine physical models with modern machine learning.

Research Interests

Battery Modeling

Physics-based and reduced-order models for battery dynamics, thermal behavior, health estimation, and parameter identification.

State-Space Models

Nonlinear and non-Gaussian state-space models for forecasting, filtering, and uncertainty-aware inference.

Ensemble Kalman Inversion

Derivative-free Bayesian learning and inverse-problem methods for complex dynamical systems.

Scientific Machine Learning

Combining neural networks, physical models, and probabilistic inference for interpretable engineering predictions.

Selected Projects
